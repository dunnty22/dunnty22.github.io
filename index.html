About

I build hardware that works in the real world.

I grew up in Anchorage, Alaska and studied Mechanical Engineering at Northeastern University, where co-ops at Stoneridge Electronics and Nuvera Fuel Cells gave me an early taste of real production environments. Since graduating in 2020 I've spent five years doing the kind of engineering that shows up on the floor — leading mechanical development of next-generation marine stabilizers at Seakeeper, and driving product and sustaining work at ALGAS SDI in Seattle.

I'm drawn to early-stage problems: the messy middle between a concept and a thing you can hold. I'm most effective when I own the whole path — from first-principles analysis through prototype, test, and production release.

Outside of work I spend time in the mountains around the Pacific Northwest and follow engineering wherever it shows up in sports and outdoor gear.
